About Me

I'm an undergaduate astrophysics researcher at Western Washington University, junior programmer, and former photographer.

Over the years of earning my Physics BS, I've had the fortune of filling a variety of roles within the department.

I've worked as a teaching assistant in a variety of introductory labs and required labs for STEM majors, as an assistant to the Lead Engineering Tech where I became familiar with a variety of equipment and software, in research where I developed what I get to ostentatiously call a method of "hyperdimensional analysis", and at the planetarium where I'm currently learning the ropes of the proprietary software and developing a users guide for new students.

At the end of this journey at Western, I'm proudly walking away with a solid foundation of understanding of the physics that govern our universe, a great deal of experience in communicating complex topics to a wide variety of people, and some deeply enriching experience through wearing as many hats as I could around the department.
