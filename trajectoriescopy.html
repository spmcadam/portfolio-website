Single-Impulse Orbital Trajectories

This page is a continuation of a homework assignment, where I have been tasked with seeing just how fast a cannonball would have to go to make it into orbit from Earth's surface.

In it's current state, the interactivity is limited to just two planets, and breaks down at very low altitudes due to the simplistic atmospheric density calculation I am using.

Explanation of parameters:

  • Vertical velocity before pitch change: how much the vertical velocity to gain from launch until the rocket begins pitching over.
  • Percentage of dV for stage 2: controls how much dV is spent for the initial stage 2 burn. The remainder will be spent after the specified coast time.
  • Coast time: how long is spent between the two burns of the second stage.
